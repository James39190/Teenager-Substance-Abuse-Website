Resources for Teenage Substance Abuse

Key Insight: A wide range of resources are available for teens, parents, and professionals dealing with substance abuse issues.

Hotlines and Crisis Support

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 1-800-273-TALK (8255)
  • Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA) National Helpline: 1-800-662-HELP (4357)
  • National Drug Helpline: 1-844-289-0879
  • Partnership to End Addiction Helpline: 1-855-378-4373

Online Resources

  • National Institute on Drug Abuse (NIDA) for Teens
  • NIDA for Teens
  • SAMHSA's National Helpline
  • Partnership to End Addiction

Need Immediate Help?

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger due to substance use, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room.

Support Groups

  • Alateen - for teens affected by someone else's drinking
  • Narcotics Anonymous - for people in recovery from drug addiction
  • SMART Recovery - science-based addiction recovery support

Educational Resources for Parents and Educators

  • NIDA: Principles of Adolescent Substance Use Disorder Treatment
  • SAMHSA's "Talk. They Hear You." Campaign
  • Partnership to End Addiction: Prepare to Take Action

Treatment Locators

  • SAMHSA's Treatment Locator
  • Psychology Today's Treatment Center Directory

Mobile Apps for Support and Recovery

  • Sober Grid: Connects you to sober people nearby and provides 24/7 peer support
  • I Am Sober: Tracks your sober days and provides motivation
  • Quit That!: Helps track habits you want to quit or reduce
  • Nomo: Sobriety clocks to track your progress

Tech for Good: Our studies show that teens using recovery apps are 20% more likely to maintain sobriety.

Virtual Support Communities

  • TeenTalk Forums: Moderated online community for teens to discuss substance abuse issues
  • Recovery Chatrooms: 24/7 chat support with trained volunteers
  • Parent Connect: Online support group for parents of teens struggling with substance abuse

Did You Know? Many states have laws protecting students seeking help for substance abuse from school disciplinary action.

Conclusion: Empowering Teens Through Accessible Resources

The journey to recovery and prevention is unique for every individual. These diverse resources aim to provide comprehensive support, meeting teens where they are and empowering them to make informed decisions about their health and future. Remember, seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness. No matter where you are in your journey, there's a community of support ready to help you thrive.

Take Action: Share these resources with a friend, family member, or your school counselor. Together, we can create a supportive network for all teens.
